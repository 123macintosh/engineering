Engineering Drawings

3.6.1 — Orthographic, isometric, and oblique projection

Engineering drawings are the universal language of engineering. They communicate the exact shape, size, and specification of a component to manufacturers worldwide.

📐 First Angle Projection (European)

In first angle projection, the object is placed between the viewer and the projection plane. The view you see is projected through the object onto the plane behind it.

Layout:

  • Front view: centre
  • Plan (top view): below the front view
  • End view from right: to the left of front view
  • End view from left: to the right of front view

Symbol: A truncated cone viewed from the narrow end (left of symbol)

📐 Third Angle Projection (American)

In third angle projection, the projection plane is between the viewer and the object. The view is projected onto the plane nearest the viewer.

Layout:

  • Front view: centre
  • Plan (top view): above the front view
  • End view from right: to the right of front view
  • End view from left: to the left of front view

Symbol: A truncated cone viewed from the wide end (right of symbol)

Complete Formula Reference

Every formula you need for the AQA 8852 written paper
⚠️ Important Note

The AQA GCSE Engineering written paper does NOT provide a formula sheet. You must memorise all formulae. This section covers every formula in the specification with worked examples.

Mechanical Systems

Gear Ratio
GR = Tdriven ÷ Tdriver
T = number of teeth
Output Speed
Nout = Nin ÷ GR
N = speed (rpm)
Mechanical Advantage
MA = Load ÷ Effort
Both in Newtons (N)
Velocity Ratio (Lever)
VR = Effort Arm ÷ Load Arm
Distances from fulcrum
Efficiency
η = (MA ÷ VR) × 100%
η = efficiency (%)
Belt Drive VR
VR = Ddriven ÷ Ddriver
D = pulley diameter (mm)

Electrical Systems

Ohm's Law
V = I × R
V=Volts · I=Amps · R=Ohms
Power
P = V × I
P=Watts · V=Volts · I=Amps
Power (alt.)
P = I² × R
P=Watts · I=Amps · R=Ohms
Charge
Q = I × t
Q=Coulombs · t=seconds
Series Resistance
RT = R₁ + R₂ + R₃
Same current through all
Parallel Resistance
1/RT = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂
Same voltage across all

Forces & Pressure

Pressure
P = F ÷ A
P=Pa · F=N · A=m²
Stress
σ = F ÷ A
σ=Pa · F=N · A=m²
Strain
ε = ΔL ÷ L₀
ε=dimensionless · ΔL=extension · L₀=original length
Young's Modulus
E = σ ÷ ε
E=Pa (GPa for metals)
Moment of Force
M = F × d
M=Nm · F=N · d=m
Pneumatic Force
F = P × A
F=N · P=Pa · A=m²

Manufacturing Calculations

Cutting Speed
v = π × D × N ÷ 1000
v=m/min · D=mm · N=rpm
Spindle Speed
N = (v × 1000) ÷ (π × D)
N=rpm · v=m/min · D=mm
Material Removal Rate
MRR = w × d × f
w=width · d=depth · f=feed (mm³/min)

Geometry & Areas

Area of Circle
A = π × r²
r = radius (m or mm)
Circumference
C = π × D = 2πr
D = diameter
Volume of Cylinder
V = π × r² × h
h = height/length

Worked Calculation Examples

Step-by-step solutions to typical exam calculations
Worked Example 1 Gear Train Calculation
A gear train consists of: Gear A (driver, 20 teeth) → Gear B (60 teeth) → Gear C (30 teeth). Gear A rotates at 1200 rpm with a torque of 5 Nm. Calculate: (a) the speed of Gear C, and (b) the torque at Gear C (assuming 100% efficiency).
Step 1
Find GR for A→B: GR₁ = T_B ÷ T_A = 60 ÷ 20 = 3
Step 2
Speed of B: N_B = 1200 ÷ 3 = 400 rpm
Step 3
Find GR for B→C: GR₂ = T_C ÷ T_B = 30 ÷ 60 = 0.5
Step 4
Speed of C: N_C = 400 ÷ 0.5 = 800 rpm
Step 5
Overall GR (A→C): GR_total = 3 × 0.5 = 1.5 (or 1200 ÷ 800 = 1.5 ✓)
Step 6
Torque at C: T_C = T_A × GR_total = 5 × 1.5 = 7.5 Nm
Answers: (a) Speed of Gear C = 800 rpm · (b) Torque at C = 7.5 Nm
Worked Example 2 Parallel Circuit Calculation
A 12V battery is connected to two resistors in parallel: R₁ = 6Ω and R₂ = 12Ω. Calculate: (a) the total resistance, (b) the total current from the battery, (c) the current through each resistor.
Step 1
Total resistance (parallel): 1/R_T = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂ = 1/6 + 1/12 = 2/12 + 1/12 = 3/12 = 1/4
Step 2
R_T = 4Ω (reciprocal of 1/4)
Step 3
Total current: I = V ÷ R_T = 12 ÷ 4 = 3 A
Step 4
Current through R₁: I₁ = V ÷ R₁ = 12 ÷ 6 = 2 A
Step 5
Current through R₂: I₂ = V ÷ R₂ = 12 ÷ 12 = 1 A
Step 6
Check: I₁ + I₂ = 2 + 1 = 3 A = total current ✓
Answers: (a) R_T = 4Ω · (b) Total current = 3A · (c) I₁ = 2A, I₂ = 1A
Worked Example 3 Pneumatic Cylinder Force
A double-acting pneumatic cylinder has a bore diameter of 80mm. The supply pressure is 5 bar. Calculate the force produced on the forward stroke.
Step 1
Convert pressure: 5 bar = 5 × 100,000 = 500,000 Pa
Step 2
Convert diameter to radius in metres: r = 80 ÷ 2 = 40mm = 0.04m
Step 3
Calculate piston area: A = π × r² = π × 0.04² = π × 0.0016 = 0.005027 m²
Step 4
Calculate force: F = P × A = 500,000 × 0.005027 = 2,513 N ≈ 2.5 kN
Answer: Force = 2,513 N (approximately 2.5 kN)
Worked Example 4 Lever Mechanical Advantage & Efficiency
A Class 1 lever has an effort arm of 600mm and a load arm of 150mm. An effort of 50N is applied. (a) Calculate the Velocity Ratio. (b) Calculate the load that can be lifted. (c) If the actual load lifted is 180N, calculate the efficiency.
Step 1
Velocity Ratio: VR = Effort Arm ÷ Load Arm = 600 ÷ 150 = 4
Step 2
Ideal load (assuming 100% efficiency): Load = Effort × VR = 50 × 4 = 200 N
Step 3
Actual MA: MA = Actual Load ÷ Effort = 180 ÷ 50 = 3.6
Step 4
Efficiency: η = (MA ÷ VR) × 100 = (3.6 ÷ 4) × 100 = 90%
Answers: (a) VR = 4 · (b) Ideal load = 200N · (c) Efficiency = 90%

QUESTION 1 1 mark
In first angle orthographic projection, where is the plan view (view from above) placed?
A Above the front view
B Below the front view
C To the left of the front view
D To the right of the front view
✓ Correct! In first angle projection, the plan view goes BELOW the front view. Remember: in 1st angle, views go OPPOSITE to where you look from.
💡 Examiner tip: 1st angle = European = plan BELOW. 3rd angle = American = plan ABOVE. The projection symbol on the drawing tells you which system is used.
QUESTION 2 2 marks
A lever has an effort arm of 400mm and a load arm of 100mm. An effort of 25N is applied. What load can be lifted (assuming 100% efficiency)?
A 6.25 N
B 100 N
C 400 N
D 25 N
✓ Correct! VR = 400 ÷ 100 = 4. At 100% efficiency, MA = VR = 4. Load = Effort × MA = 25 × 4 = 100 N.
💡 Examiner tip: Always show: (1) VR formula and calculation, (2) MA = VR at 100% efficiency, (3) Load = Effort × MA. Show all working for full marks.
QUESTION 3 1 mark
Which type of line is used to show hidden detail in an engineering drawing?
A Continuous thin line
B Short dashed line
C Chain-dash line
D Continuous thick line
✓ Correct! Hidden detail (features not visible in that view) is shown using short dashed lines. Chain-dash lines are used for centre lines.
💡 Examiner tip: Thick continuous = visible outlines. Thin continuous = dimension/extension lines, hatching. Dashed = hidden detail. Chain-dash = centre lines.
QUESTION 4 2 marks
Two resistors, 4Ω and 12Ω, are connected in series to a 16V battery. What is the current flowing through the circuit?
A 4 A
B 1 A
C 0.25 A
D 2 A
✓ Correct! R_total = 4 + 12 = 16Ω. I = V ÷ R = 16 ÷ 16 = 1 A.
💡 Examiner tip: Series: R_total = R₁ + R₂. Parallel: 1/R_T = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂. Always find total resistance first, then use Ohm's Law.
QUESTION 5 1 mark
What does the tolerance of a dimension tell you?
A The exact size the part must be
B The acceptable range of variation in a dimension
C The material the part is made from
D The maximum size the part can be
✓ Correct! Tolerance is the total acceptable range of variation: tolerance = upper limit − lower limit. For Ø25 ±0.05mm, tolerance = 25.05 − 24.95 = 0.10mm.
💡 Examiner tip: Tolerance = upper limit − lower limit. Tighter tolerances cost more to achieve and require more precise measuring equipment.

★★★ Hard Style: AQA 8852 Multi-part Calculation 6 marks
A CNC milling machine is used to machine an aluminium component. The recommended cutting speed for aluminium is 300 m/min. The milling cutter has a diameter of 25mm.

(a) Calculate the required spindle speed in rpm. Give your answer to the nearest whole number. [2 marks]
(b) The cutter has 4 teeth and the feed per tooth is 0.1mm. Calculate the feed rate in mm/min. [2 marks]
(c) State TWO advantages of using CNC milling over manual milling for producing 200 identical components. [2 marks]
Model Answer (6 marks)
(a) N = (v × 1000) ÷ (π × D) (1)
N = (300 × 1000) ÷ (π × 25) = 300,000 ÷ 78.54 = 3,820 rpm (1)

(b) Feed rate = feed per tooth × number of teeth × spindle speed (1)
Feed rate = 0.1 × 4 × 3820 = 1,528 mm/min (1)

(c) Any two of (1 mark each):
• Consistent quality — every component identical (1)
• Can run unattended / 24 hours (1)
• Higher accuracy / tighter tolerances maintained (1)
• Faster production rate (1)
• Reduced skill requirement during production (1)
💡 Examiner tip: Always write the formula before substituting values. Show all working — even if your final answer is wrong, you may get method marks. Round only at the final step.

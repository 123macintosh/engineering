Engineering Systems

Systems thinking is at the heart of engineering. This lesson covers mechanical systems (gears, levers, cams, pulleys), electrical and electronic systems (circuits, logic gates, sensors), structural systems, and pneumatics — all with interactive exercises and real exam questions.

  • Construct and evaluate truth tables for logic gates (AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR)
  • Describe pneumatic components and circuits

Input–Process–Output (IPO) Model

3.3.1 — The foundation of systems thinking

Every engineering system can be described using the Input–Process–Output model. Some systems also include feedback — where the output is monitored and used to adjust the input to maintain a desired state.

Mechanical Systems

3.3.2 — Gears, levers, cams, pulleys, linkages

Gears

Gears are toothed wheels that mesh together to transmit rotational motion and torque. They can change speed, torque, and direction of rotation.

DIAGRAM — Gear Train (Label the parts)
📐 Gear Ratio Formulae — MUST KNOW
Gear Ratio
GR = Tdriven ÷ Tdriver
T = number of teeth
Output Speed
Nout = Nin ÷ GR
N = speed in rpm
Output Torque
Tout = Tin × GR
T = torque in Nm

Worked Example: Driver gear has 15 teeth, driven gear has 45 teeth. Input speed = 900 rpm.
GR = 45 ÷ 15 = 3:1. Output speed = 900 ÷ 3 = 300 rpm. Torque is multiplied by 3.

Levers

A lever is a rigid bar that pivots about a fulcrum. Levers are classified into three orders (classes) based on the position of the fulcrum, effort, and load.

DIAGRAM — Three Classes of Lever
📐 Mechanical Advantage & Velocity Ratio
Mechanical Advantage
MA = Load ÷ Effort
MA > 1 = force multiplied
Velocity Ratio (Lever)
VR = Effort Arm ÷ Load Arm
Distances from fulcrum

Efficiency: η = (MA ÷ VR) × 100%. A perfect lever has η = 100% (no friction losses). Real levers are always <100%.

Cams & Followers

🔄 Cams

A cam is an irregularly shaped rotating disc that converts rotary motion → reciprocating (up-down) motion. The shape of the cam determines the motion of the follower.

  • Pear-shaped cam: Follower has a period of dwell (rest) at top and bottom
  • Eccentric cam: Follower has smooth, continuous up-down motion (like a sine wave)
  • Snail/drop cam: Follower rises slowly then drops suddenly

Uses: Car engine valves, sewing machines, printing presses, automatic machinery

Pulleys

⚙️ Pulley Systems

A single fixed pulley changes the direction of force but provides no mechanical advantage (MA = 1).

A block and tackle (multiple pulleys) provides mechanical advantage. The MA equals the number of rope sections supporting the load.

VR = number of rope sections supporting the load

Belt and pulley systems transmit motion between shafts. The velocity ratio = diameter of driven ÷ diameter of driver.

📐 Belt Drive Calculations
Velocity Ratio (Belt Drive)
VR = Ddriven ÷ Ddriver
D = pulley diameter (mm)

Example: Driver pulley = 50mm, driven = 200mm. VR = 200 ÷ 50 = 4. Output speed is ¼ of input speed.

Circuit Fundamentals

📐 Key Electrical Formulae — MUST KNOW
Ohm's Law
V = I × R
V=Volts · I=Amps · R=Ohms(Ω)
Power
P = V × I
P=Watts · V=Volts · I=Amps
Charge
Q = I × t
Q=Coulombs · t=seconds
Series Resistance
Rtotal = R₁ + R₂ + R₃
Same current through all
Parallel Resistance
1/RT = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂
Same voltage across all

Logic Gates

Logic gates are electronic components that perform Boolean logic operations. They are the building blocks of all digital electronics and computers. Each gate has one or more inputs and one output. Inputs and outputs are either HIGH (1) or LOW (0).

📐 Pneumatic Force Calculation
Force from Cylinder
F = P × A
F=Force(N) · P=Pressure(Pa) · A=Area(m²)

Example: A cylinder with bore diameter 50mm (radius 0.025m) at 6 bar (600,000 Pa).
A = π × r² = π × 0.025² = 0.00196 m². F = 600,000 × 0.00196 = 1,178 N

QUESTION 1 1 mark
A driver gear has 12 teeth and a driven gear has 48 teeth. What is the gear ratio?
A 1:4
B 4:1
C 12:48
D 2:1
✓ Correct! GR = T_driven ÷ T_driver = 48 ÷ 12 = 4. The gear ratio is 4:1, meaning the driven gear rotates at ¼ the speed but with 4× the torque.
💡 Examiner tip: Always express gear ratio as driven:driver or as a single number (4). Show your working for calculation questions.
QUESTION 2 1 mark
An AND gate has inputs A=1 and B=0. What is the output Q?
A 0
B 1
C Depends on previous state
D Cannot be determined
✓ Correct! An AND gate outputs 1 ONLY when BOTH inputs are 1. Since B=0, the output is 0.
💡 Examiner tip: AND = "all inputs must be 1". OR = "at least one input must be 1". NOT = "opposite of input".
QUESTION 3 1 mark
A wheelbarrow is an example of which class of lever?
A Class 1 — fulcrum between load and effort
B Class 2 — load between fulcrum and effort
C Class 3 — effort between fulcrum and load
D Class 4 — load at the fulcrum
✓ Correct! In a wheelbarrow: the wheel is the fulcrum, the load is in the tray (between fulcrum and effort), and the effort is applied at the handles. This is Class 2.
💡 Examiner tip: Class 1 = scissors, crowbar, see-saw. Class 2 = wheelbarrow, nutcracker. Class 3 = tweezers, fishing rod, forearm.
QUESTION 4 1 mark
A circuit has a 12V battery and a 6Ω resistor. What current flows?
A 0.5 A
B 2 A
C 72 A
D 18 A
✓ Correct! Using Ohm's Law: I = V ÷ R = 12 ÷ 6 = 2 A.
💡 Examiner tip: Rearrange V = IR as needed: I = V/R, R = V/I. Always show the formula, substitution, and answer with units.
QUESTION 5 1 mark
Which component has a resistance that DECREASES as temperature INCREASES?
A LDR (Light Dependent Resistor)
B NTC Thermistor
C Fixed resistor
D Diode
✓ Correct! An NTC (Negative Temperature Coefficient) thermistor has resistance that decreases as temperature increases. An LDR decreases with light, not temperature.
💡 Examiner tip: NTC = Negative Temperature Coefficient = resistance falls with temperature. LDR = resistance falls with light. Both are used as sensors in potential divider circuits.

★★★ Hard Style: AQA 8852 Calculation + Explain 5 marks
A gear train has three gears: Gear A (driver, 10 teeth) meshes with Gear B (30 teeth), which meshes with Gear C (15 teeth). Gear A rotates at 600 rpm.

(a) Calculate the speed of Gear B. [2 marks]
(b) Calculate the speed of Gear C. [2 marks]
(c) State the direction of rotation of Gear C relative to Gear A. [1 mark]
Model Answer (5 marks)
(a) GR(A→B) = T_B ÷ T_A = 30 ÷ 10 = 3 (1)
Speed of B = 600 ÷ 3 = 200 rpm (1)

(b) GR(B→C) = T_C ÷ T_B = 15 ÷ 30 = 0.5 (1)
Speed of C = 200 ÷ 0.5 = 400 rpm (1)
[OR: Overall GR = 30/10 × 15/30 = 1.5, Speed C = 600/1.5 = 400 rpm]

(c) Gear C rotates in the same direction as Gear A. (1)
[Each meshing pair reverses direction. A→B reverses, B→C reverses again = same as A]
💡 Examiner tip: For gear train direction — each mesh point reverses direction. An even number of meshes = same direction as driver. An odd number = opposite direction. Always show the gear ratio calculation before finding speed.
